October 2018

Centre for Governance, Institutions, and Organisations (CGIO)

Project Intern and Research Analyst
Corporate Governance, Sustainability Reporting, CSR, Qualitative Research, Data Entry, Report Writing

Written in collaboration with WWF, the "Sustainable Banking in ASEAN" report evaluated corporate governance practices' of major banks in 6 ASEAN countries. The evaluation was done per scorecards designed by CGIO and WWF, and based on international standards such as UNGC, respective countries' CG Code, and those issued by Bank of International Settlements. I was brought into the project as a writer from the CGIO side, and I was to represent the collective analysis results of 5 other student analysts who had been coding annual and sustainability reports of the evaluation's subjects. The report was an extension to its previous year installation.

I also assisted in the writing of other in-house publications: "Sustainability Reporting in Singapore 2018" and "Sustainability Reporting in ASEAN 2018" (done in collaboration with ASEAN CSR Network). This project looked into the sustainability reporting landscape in the respective regions and evaluated listed companies' sustainability reports following pre-designed scorecards. The scorecards in this project also heavily followed industry-recognised standards such as GRI and DJSI. ​

This opportunity has allowed me to hone my research skills and learn to be resourceful; in our attempts to draw analysis between our findings and the respective regions and countries' regulatory landscapes, I was able to lend my language skill to localise Indonesian legislative documents that allowed us to be caught up with Indonesia's latest corporate governance and sustainability reporting trends. In overall, writing about subjects that I had no background in didn't diminish my willingness to internalise myself fully into the topics. I feel it's within my sense of ownership and stewardship to ensure the materials that I delivered could add value to the intended audience. My promotion from a student author to a co-author was just a plus from the internalised efforts put in.
